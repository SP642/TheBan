The World's Most Luxurious Banana

LIFESTYLE | by SizzlingPotassium642

 

A yellow dream on four wheels, this is the yellow Rolls-Royce Phantom we’ve chosen to write about.
Starting from 1925, a history of almost a century from the first Phantom I, also known as 40/50 Phantom and successor of 40/50, the famous Silver Ghost.

The Rolls-Royce Phantom is not a just a car it's a full-sized luxury saloon car launched in 2003.
The body is mostly aluminium, extrusions that are used to construct the aluminium spaceframe are produced in Norway using hydroelectric power, shaped and machined in Denmark and finally hand-welded in Germany.
Thanks to this piece of engineering, a boundless set of gadgets and comfort has been developed and they are today more accessible on various common car models.

 

 

 

The luxurious frivolities vary over time from a navigation system with voice recognition, power sunroof, upgraded leather upholstery, rear-view camera, rear-seat DVD entertainment system, continuing to 15-speaker Lexicon Logic7 premium sound system, 8-disc CD changer, 18-way power front seats, 16-way power rear seats, heated and cooled cup holders, rear-seat tables, universal garage door opener, until you get to power tilt/telescopic heated wood and leather-wrapped steering wheel with radio, climate, and navigation controls, power open/close boot lid, power closing doors, wireless headphones, iPod adapter, refrigerator, and air conditioning with 5-zone climate controls.

Were you wondering? Yes, it also has an engine. Take a look at tables below.
